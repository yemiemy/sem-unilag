Welcome to SEM UNILAG

Shell Eco-Marathon University of Lagos (SEM Unilag) is a team of students in the University of Lagos, who innovate for the future of energy in Africa and in the world. We represent the university on the global scale at the Shell Eco-marathon competition.

Learn More

A FEW WORDS ABOUT US

In 2013, Students of the University of Lagos and 3 other Nigerian universities were sponsored by Shell Nigeria to attend the Shell Eco-Marathon drivers’ world championship regional in the Netherlands. This provided a direction for the students, whose initial drive was “building exciting technology” and thus, the Shell Eco-Marathon University of Lagos Team was formed. Inspired by the display of innovation beyond anything they had previously imagined, the team, ‘Shell Eco-Marathon Unilag’, entered the competition in 2014.

Following years of handing over the baton, the team today boasts just under 30 engineers of various disciplines whose goal is to bring the Nigerian perspective to the table in the global race for automotive energy efficiency. Each successive set of the team is tasked with improving on the innovation of previous sets, with the overall goal of building a vehicle to participate in the annual event.

As a long term goal, the University of Lagos team aims to diversify its scope to include other projects centred on the application of energy efficient technology, especially as relevant to needs in local communities. At Shell Eco-Marathon in the University of Lagos, the priority is to drive innovation in energy efficient technology whilst fostering hands-on technical experience for students.

To achieve its goal, the University of Lagos team comprises student volunteers in Subteams occupying technical and non-technical roles, led by other students under the general supervision of staff advisers from the Faculty of Engineering.

WHAT WE DO

Shell Eco-Marathon is an annual global competition for high school and university student teams, which provides a collaborative platform to foster hands-on experience amongst young innovators in achieving automotive ultra-energy-efficiency. Around the world, the programme inspires thousands of students to work collaboratively to put their theories of energy efficiency to the test, using cutting-edge technology, critical thinking, and innovative ideas. The competition features vehicles in 2 categories (Prototype and UrbanConcept) and 3 energy types; internal combustion engine, battery-electric, and hydrogen fuel cell.

THE STORY SO FAR: OUR VEHICLE PROJECTS

The University of Lagos team has competed twice in the global event since representatives of the university were invited to witness the future of automotive energy efficiency (Netherlands, 2013). On both occasions, the team competed in the prototype battery-electric category and passed the first two stages of the competition to qualify for the drivers’ world championship race in the third stage.

THE AUTONOV II

The Autonov II was the first ever SEM Unilag vehicle. It featured a fibreglass body – cum steel frame configuration. Construction for the Autonov II (a prototype battery-electric vehicle) began in 2013 and was completed in 2014, after which it was entered for Shell Eco-marathon Europe in The Netherlands. When completed, the vehicle weighed 96kg and attained a top speed of 25km/hr. The vehicle was painted partly green to champion the Nigerian spirit of innovation.

THE AUTONOV III (A3)

In 2015, Team Unilag once again completed a prototype battery-electric vehicle and entered it in Shell Eco-marathon Africa. The Autonov III, like its predecessor, was built with a fibreglass body and a steel frame. The completed vehicle weighed 65kg, which was 31kg lighter than the Autonov II. In Johannesburg that year, the A3 went on to achieve a top speed of 40km/hr and an energy efficiency of 120.1km/Kwh, the then equivalent of driving from Lagos to Ibadan on only ₦12.54 worth of electricity! The Autonov III was an all-round improvement in performance relative to the Autonov II.

THE AUTONOV DELUXE (AD ‘20)

In 2016, the SEM Unilag Team began building what has become our legacy vehicle. Spurred by innovation in other vehicle categories, the team ventured into uncharted waters by building its first UrbanConcept, gasoline powered vehicle. The Autonov Deluxe came to represent the rebirth of SEM Unilag and in 2019, the team successfully scaled through the Shell Eco-marathon registration. In July 2020, SEM Unilag was slated to represent the University of Lagos with the AD’20 at Shell Eco-marathon Europe (Mercedes Benz World). However, due to the Covid-19 crisis, the event was cancelled and work towards completing the vehicle was put on hold.

EXTENDING INNOVATION: OUR OTHER PROJECTS

THE SEM UNILAG HUB

In 2020, the team observed that whilst actual membership numbers had to be limited, there existed an entire community of students and energy efficiency enthusiasts that could be inspired to pursue the future of energy in Africa. Thus was the SEM Unilag Hub born; an initiative to bring energy efficiency education to Nigerian youth. Community numbers grew to over 200 between February and May 2020.

ENERGY EFFICIENCY PROJECTS

Inspired by the energy efficiency theme of the Shell Eco-marathon competition and the Shell initiative to #MakeTheFuture, SEM Unilag launched plans for its first community-driven project in 2020, a portable solar generator designed for Nigerian households.

Teams

At SEM Unilag, we work in Subteams coordinated by a central leadership. There are 8 Subteams; 4 technical and 4 non-technical. Our Faculty Advisor and Team Manager oversee 8 Subteam Heads, who in turn coordinate the activities of team members under them. Our internal structure has been refined over the years to be both effective and efficient!
